## Tutor Comment
Great work on assignment 2, well done!

While you have made good use of some objects, it would have been great to see use of a class to represent the board state
so as to avoid slow string manipulation - in large projects such as
this an initial investment into developing a strong structure to represent the game often makes later alterations
far simpler to implement. However, in lieu of this you have made excellent progress with completion of many tasks and
the underlying logic and data structures used are excellent. For task 10, while your approach is correct, it is far
more efficient to use recursion for search problems. Check out some of the examples from lectures/labs/past exams
for some examples.

Great work on the GUI component of the assignment also! I do not believe your game.jar is the most up to date version
of your board class, I have marked your board class and made a small deduction from your game.jar, please let me know if
you would rather I mark the other way around. While there are various minor bugs your implementation is in general 
intuitive and attractive, well done.
